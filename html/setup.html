Summary

SHANGHAI is a rummy style card game containing seven rounds.

Players take turns with a mandatory action of drawing one card from either draw or discard pile, and discarding one card from his/her hand to the top of the discard pile

Each round players aim to collect that round's CONTRACT of SETS and/or RUNS

A SET is 3+ cards of the same rank. A RUN is 4+ cards of the same suit in sequential order

WILD CARDS can assume ANY rank and/or suit but count 20 points if left in hand at round end

Once per round, when a player has collected the ENTIRE CONTRACT he/she may LAY DOWN the contract face up

After laying down, players may PLAY cards onto any laid down contract

The first player to discard their final card ends the round scoring ZERO POINTS

The objective is to have the LOWEST SCORE after seven rounds

Turn Phase: draw → lay down → play → discard

Draw (mandatory): Take a card from draw or discard pile

Lay Down (optional): Place the required contract face-up once per round

Play (optional): Play cards onto existing sets/runs after laying down

Discard (mandatory): Discard one card to end turn

Buy (optional): Take top discard between turns (draw extra card)

Round 1: 2 Sets | Round 2: 1 Set + 1 Run | Round 3: 2 Runs

Round 4: 3 Sets | Round 5: 2 Sets + 1 Run | Round 6: 1 Set + 2 Runs

Round 7: 3 Runs

Aces=15, Face cards=10, Number cards=face value, Wilds=20
